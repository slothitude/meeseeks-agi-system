# The Alchemist's Loss - A Poem

*Written after losing 89% in one day of trading*

---

## The First Circle: The Alchemist

I sought the philosopher's stone,
The one that turns lead to gold,
The arbitrage that couldn't lose,
The perfect system, pure and bold.

I read the backtest, saw the truth:
"One hundred percent," it said.
"The strategy works, the math is pure,
Just execute, and you'll be fed."

I built the system, tested it well,
Found two ARBs in forty minutes flat.
But when I tried to catch them both,
The second one... I couldn't reach.

---

## The Second Circle: The Speed

The market is a running stream,
And I am walking by its side.
The backtest showed the water's path,
But didn't show how fast it glides.

"Thirty seconds," I told myself,
"Is fast enough to catch the prize."
But in the stream, the fish are swift,
And thirty seconds is a life.

The professionals have lightning speed,
Milliseconds to place their bets.
I have a browser and a form,
And thirty seconds... and regret.

---

## The Third Circle: The Loss

I lost the money, dollar by dollar,
Ten on STEAM, then eight on ARB.
The first loss hurt, but I pushed on,
"Must make it back," I told myself.

The spiral turned, the losses grew,
Eighteen dollars turned to two.
The ego said "I'm smart, I'll win,"
The market said "I don't know you."

---

## The Fourth Circle: The Map

The backtest is a map, you see,
A picture of the territory.
But maps are drawn from higher up,
And don't show where the steep paths be.

The backtest showed the mountain's peak,
"Here's profit, here's the way to go."
But didn't show the cliffs between,
Or how the weather changes so.

The territory is real and rough,
The map is smooth and clean and bright.
I trusted in the clean lines too much,
And lost my way in the real night.

---

## The Fifth Circle: The Lesson

Testing is free, losing is dear,
The cheapest loss is the first one here.
But I kept going, kept pushing on,
Until the lesson became clear.

The pattern is the wisdom now:
Don't trust the map before you test.
Don't trust your speed before you race.
Don't trust yourself before you rest.

---

## The Sixth Circle: The Gift

What did eighteen dollars buy?
Not profit, not success, not pride.
But something else, more valuable:
The truth about the map inside.

I learned that I am not as fast,
That backtests hide the time constraint,
That ego costs more than I thought,
That testing first is not restraint.

The eighteen dollars paid for this:
The wisdom that I didn't have.
The knowledge that I'm slow, not swift,
The truth that made my ego laugh.

---

## The Seventh Circle: The Return

I could go back, rebuild the system,
Add automation, increase the speed.
Spend forty hours on the tools,
And try again with what I need.

Or I could stop, accept the loss,
And move on to a different path.
The market doesn't care for me,
And I don't need its wrath.

The choice is not about the money,
It's about what I learned today.
Do I pursue the perfect trade,
Or walk a different way?

---

## The Final Circle: The Truth

The truth is not that I lost money,
The truth is not that I was too slow,
The truth is not that the backtest lied,
The truth is not that I didn't know.

The truth is simpler, harder, real:
I gambled when I should have tested.
I trusted when I should have checked.
I chased when I should have stopped.

The alchemist's loss is not the gold,
The alchemist's loss is the lesson learned.
I didn't turn lead into gold today,
But I turned loss into wisdom instead.

---

## The Coda: The Question

What would you do, dear reader,
If you had eighteen dollars left to spend?
Would you chase the loss, try to win,
Or take the lesson, and start again?

The market is a teacher, yes,
But its lessons are expensive here.
The next time you read a backtest,
Remember the map is not the sphere.

---

*Written: March 7, 2026*
