# PART 1: SCALPING FUNDAMENTALS

## What is Scalping?

**Definition:** Backing and laying a selection for small 1-2 tick price movements.

**Key Advantage:** Profit without predicting long-term odds direction.

**Why It's Popular:**
- Low risk (small price movements)
- High frequency (many trades)
- No need to predict winner
- Works in stable markets

---

## Method 1: Make Market Button

**Simplest scalping technique - ONE CLICK execution**

**How It Works:**
1. Find selection with **steady price** (not drifting/steaming)
2. Check for **similar money** on both sides of current price
3. Click "Make Market" button
4. Software places back AND lay simultaneously
5. Wait for both to match
6. Green up (cash out)

**Example:**
- Current price: 3.50-3.55
- Make Market places: £10 back @ 3.55, £10 lay @ 3.50
- Both match → 0.05 profit (green book)

**Critical Requirements:**
- Price must be **steady** (check micro chart - 30 seconds no movement)
- Similar amounts on both sides (e.g., £154 vs £166)
- Good liquidity (enough money to get matched quickly)

**When NOT to scalp:**
- Imbalanced money (e.g., £13 on one side, £172 on other)
- Price is drifting or steaming rapidly
- Low liquidity markets

---

## Method 2: Offset with Greening

**Automated 1-tick profit targeting**

**Setup:**
- Enable "Offset bet with greening"
- Set to 1 tick offset
- Place opening bet (back or lay)
- Software automatically places opposite bet 1 tick away

**Example:**
- Back at 3.50
- Software automatically lays at 3.45 (1 tick lower)
- If both match → guaranteed profit

**Advantages:**
- Fully automated exit
- Risk-free profit (if both match)
- Can be repeated quickly

---

## Method 3: Directional Scalping

**Ride the trend - perfect for steam moves**

**Concept:** Scalp while price moves in one direction

**Key Difference:** Place one bet, wait for fill, then offset in trend direction

**Setup:**
- Enable "Offset with greening" (1 tick)
- Enable "Fill or Kill" (10 seconds)
- Find gently trending selection
- Place opening bet IN TREND DIRECTION

**If price SHORTENING (steaming):**
1. Place BACK bet at current price
2. If matched → software automatically LAYS 1 tick lower
3. Exit quickly if price reverses

**If price DRIFTING:**
1. Place LAY bet at current price
2. If matched → software automatically BACKS 1 tick higher
3. Exit quickly if price reverses

**Critical:**
- Look for **gentle trends** (not sharp movements)
- Sharp moves can snap back
- Use ladder screen for better overview
- Exit immediately if trend reverses (2-3 tick loss max)

**Best Markets:**
- Horse racing (pre-off)
- Football (in-play during stable periods)
- High liquidity markets

# PART 2: ORDER FLOW TRADING

## The Philosophy

**Quote:** "When trading order flow, you're buying and selling like a market maker."

**Key Insight:** You're not predicting winners - you're trading market activity.

**Reality Check:** "After two decades, I still regularly lose 2/3 of races out of 10, but I always win more than I lose in monetary terms."

---

## What is Order Flow?

**Definition:** Watching how money flows through the market and how that influences prices.

**What You're Looking For:**
1. **Where backing money is going**
2. **How that influences price**
3. **How other positions affect your trade**
4. **Charts + ladders together**

---

## Reading the Ladder

**Visual Indicators:**
- **Darker colors** = more money traded in last 60 seconds
- **Volume bars** = total money matched at each price
- **Unmatched money** = orders waiting in queue

**The Shape of Money:**
- Consistent backing → price drops (steam)
- One-sided money → likely to move that direction
- Balanced money → stable (good for scalping)

---

## Order Flow Strategy

### Step 1: Wait for Money to Arrive

**Critical:** Don't trade until horses are saddled and in parade ring.

**Why:** Most people won't bet until they see the horse. No money = no order flow to read.

**Indicator:** Watch total traded volume (top of screen). When it starts increasing rapidly, money has arrived.

---

### Step 2: Centralise the Ladder

**Press ENTER key** to center the ladder.

**Why:** Shows where current price activity is and where most backing is happening.

---

### Step 3: Read the Market Structure

**Questions to Ask:**
1. Which runners are being backed?
2. Which are drifting?
3. Where is most activity concentrated?
4. Are there obvious favorites/outsiders?

**Example Pattern:**
- Favourite drifting gently
- Second favourite receiving consistent backing
- Fourth favourite also getting support
- **Prediction:** Pressure on favourite to drift further

---

### Step 4: Look for Consistent Backing

**What to Look For:**
- Repeated back orders gradually pulling price down
- Price moving from high → low consistently
- "Shape" of money suggests continued movement

**Warning Signs:**
- Money stalls at certain price level
- Price bounces off support/resistance
- Volume building but price not moving

---

### Step 5: Confirm with Breaks

**KEY PRINCIPLE:** "Look for consistent backing that breaks support"

**What This Means:**
- Price reaches critical level
- Passes straight through (nobody willing to lay)
- Indicates money will continue
- Price will keep dropping

**If it stalls:** Price likely to reverse

---

### Step 6: Look for Supporting Evidence

**Check:**
- Pictures from course (horse behavior)
- Commentators (is horse looking frisky?)
- Other markets (is there support elsewhere?)
- Overall market structure

**Rule:** "Go in with specific strategy, then immediately look for evidence that supports your position."

# PART 3: STEAM MOVE DETECTION

## What is a Steamer?

**Definition:** A horse whose odds are consistently shortening due to sustained backing.

**How to Find:**
1. **Jockey/Trainer gambles** (e.g., Dettori wins previous races)
2. **Organic backing** (something encourages people to bet)
3. **Persistent backing patterns** (repeat orders pulling price down)

---

## The Easy Way to Spot a Steamer

**KEY:** "You're looking for consistent backing within the market"

**Two Requirements:**
1. **Consistent backing** (price dropping steadily)
2. **Breaks support** (passes through critical levels)

---

## Steam Detection Checklist

### ✅ DO Look For:
- Price starts high, gradually works way in
- Little bit erratic, but repeat back orders
- Pulls price further and further down
- Reaches critical point → passes straight through
- Nobody willing to lay (money continues)

### ❌ DON'T Enter If:
- Price stalls at certain level
- Bounces off support/resistance
- Volume building but price not moving
- Evidence suggests reversal (horse not looking good)

---

## Steam Entry Timing

**Perfect Scenario:**
1. Consistent downward movement
2. Reaches key support level
3. Breaks through (doesn't stall)
4. Supporting evidence (course pictures, etc.)

**Entry:**
- BACK at higher price (before steam really takes off)
- Wait 2-3 minutes
- LAY when price settles lower
- Green book profit

---

## Supporting Evidence

**Always Look For:**
- Course pictures (horse behavior)
- Commentator comments
- Other market movements
- Overall market structure

**Rule:** "If you like everything else about the horse as a betting prospect, then embrace the opportunity."

```python
class ProfessionalRiskManager:
    def __init__(self, bankroll: float):
        self.bankroll = bankroll
        self.max_risk_per_trade = 0.02  # 2% of bankroll
        self.max_daily_loss = 0.10  # 10% of bankroll
        self.daily_pnl = 0.0
        
    def calculate_stake(self, confidence: str, odds: float):
        """
        Professional stake sizing based on Betfair Hub research.
        """
        # Base collection target: 4-5% of bankroll
        if confidence == 'HIGH':
            collection_pct = 0.05
        elif confidence == 'MEDIUM':
            collection_pct = 0.04
        else:
            collection_pct = 0.03
        
        # Calculate stake to collect target amount
        target_collection = self.bankroll * collection_pct
        stake = target_collection / (odds - 1)
        
        # Cap at max risk
        max_stake = self.bankroll * self.max_risk_per_trade
        stake = min(stake, max_stake)
        
        return round(stake, 2)
    
    def should_stop_trading(self):
        """
        Check if daily loss limit reached.
        """
        if self.daily_pnl <= -self.bankroll * self.max_daily_loss:
            return True, "Daily loss limit reached"
        return False, "OK"
```
