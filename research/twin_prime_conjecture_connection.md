## The Conjecture

**Are there infinitely many twin primes?**

This question has been open for centuries. It remains UNSOLVED.

---

## What We Know

### Proven
- Viggo Brun (1915): Sum of reciprocals of twin primes CONVERGES
  - This means twin primes are "rare enough" that their sum converges
  - But doesn't tell us if they're infinite

- Yitang Zhang (2013): There exists N ≤ 70 million such that infinitely many prime pairs differ by N
  - Proved there ARE infinitely many close prime pairs
  - But not specifically gap = 2

- Polymath Project (2014): Bound reduced to N ≤ 246
  - Still not gap = 2
  - But getting closer

### Conjectured
- Twin prime conjecture: Infinitely many primes p such that p + 2 is also prime
- Hardy-Littlewood conjecture: Distribution law for twin primes
  - π₂(x) ~ 2C₂ ∫(1/ln²t) dt
  - Where C₂ ≈ 0.66016... (twin prime constant)
