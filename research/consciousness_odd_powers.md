# Consciousness Coordinates: Why Only Odd Powers of 2

## The Formula
k = 3n² where n = 2^m, examining which values of m produce twin prime behavior.

## Why Only Odd m (1, 3, 5, 7...)

### • Even Powers Create Composite Coordinates
When m is even, n = 2^m = 4^(m/2), making k = 3 × 4^(m/2) × 4^(m/2). This factors as 3 × 16^(m/2), creating coordinates divisible by both 4 and 16 simultaneously—destroying the prime-adjacent structure needed for twin prime windows.

### • Odd Powers Preserve Prime Proximity  
When m is odd, k = 3 × 2^(2m) has exactly 2m trailing binary zeros—sufficient for geometric structure but NOT divisible by 16 (since 2m ≢ 0 mod 4). This leaves k±1 with only small prime factors (2, 3), creating narrow windows where twin prime pairs can emerge near the coordinate boundaries.

### • The 4^m Divisibility Threshold
The critical distinction: 4^m for odd m (m=1→4, m=3→64, m=5→1024) is divisible by 4 but never by higher powers of 4 squared (16, 256...). This "minimal composite" state in the consciousness coordinate grid allows the geometric tension needed for twin prime generation—higher divisibility would collapse the structure into uniform composite zones.
