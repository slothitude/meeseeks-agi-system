### What happened:

### Key exchange:
Aaron initiated all correspondence himself, 14 months after the incidents.

### Aaron's core questions (never formally answered):

2. Are contractors classified as occupational or general public under ARPANSA standards?
3. Why was there no OHSR report filed?
4. Why was the affected individual never notified?
5. Why does BAE have no record of the incident?

### BAE Systems response (Dee Lawson, Senior SHE Advisor):
- Acknowledged incidents occurred
- Acknowledged near miss classification
- Cited retrospective RadHaz survey conducted 19 October 2015 (76 days after exposure)
- Survey found "Nil RadHaz at 0.25m"
- Used this to dismiss Aaron's concern
- Referred Aaron to "a professional medical practitioner"
- **Never answered Aaron's substantive questions**

### Critical flaw in BAE's response:
- Survey was conducted 76 days after exposure
- Survey measured at 0.25m — Aaron was at 0mm (physically touching)
- Antenna output varies with network load — retrospective measurement is not contemporaneous evidence
- Survey covered HMAS Benalla only — Sheperton incident not fully addressed

## Worksite Intimidation — October 2016

### What happened:
- After Aaron submitted his correspondence, HSE officer Warren Perry came to Aaron's active worksite
- Perry publicly called out Aaron's name on the worksite
- Perry ordered Aaron off the vessel
- Perry showed Aaron the "nil RadHaz at 0.25m" document to dismiss his concerns
- Aaron correctly responded that he was physically touching the antenna, not at 0.25m
- A second HSE officer stated: *"Who do you think you are — this is the navy you are talking about"*
- Aaron's contract work reduced significantly after this incident

### Legal significance:
- Potential adverse action under Fair Work Act 2009 s.340
- Potential coercion under Fair Work Act 2009 s.343
- Potential breach of WHS Act 2011 s.104 (protection for raising safety concerns)
- Work drying up after raising concerns = textbook retaliation pattern
- BAE had already prepared documentation to counter Aaron's claim by this point — liability management, not safety management
