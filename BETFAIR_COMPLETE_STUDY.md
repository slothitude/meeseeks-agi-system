# PART 1: STAKING & MONEY MANAGEMENT

## The 65/35 Rule

**Professional insight:** The difference between winning and losing punters is:
- **35%** related to race analysis and selections
- **65%** related to betting habits and staking

**Key Principle:** Poor staking will destroy even the best selection system.

---

## 6 Rules of Professional Staking

### Rule 1: Establish a Betting Bank
- Dedicated capital for betting only
- Working capital, not gambling money
- Can be $500, $1,000, $5,000 or more
- Should have at least 50% to start

### Rule 2: Realistic Expectations
- Can't turn $1,000 into $2,000 in a day
- Unrealistic expectations → impatience → poor decisions
- Build slowly, compound growth

### Rule 3: Balance Profit and Risk

**Objective:** "Maximise real profit dollars within a risk threshold you are comfortable with."

**The Approach:**
- Bet to **COLLECT 4-5% of your betting bank**
- Based on price received from market
- Proportional to winning chance

**Examples ($10,000 bank, collect $400):**
- $2.00 odds → stake $200
- $3.50 odds → stake $114
- $4.00 odds → stake $100
- $8.00 odds → stake $50
- $20.00 odds → stake $20

**Why It Works:**
- Maximises bankroll growth
- Moderate risk level
- Comfortable bet sizes
- Survives losing runs

### Rule 4: Level Staking
- Same amount on each bet
- Higher variance (bigger peaks and troughs)
- Works better at short end of market

**Level Stake Guide (by strike rate):**

| Strike Rate | Level Stake % of Bank |
|-------------|----------------------|
| 50% | 2.0% |
| 40% | 1.7% |
| 30% | 1.3% |
| 25% | 1.1% |
| 20% | 0.9% |

**Lay Betting:** Liability of 5-6% of bankroll

### Rule 5: Move With Your Bank
- Adjust bet size as bank grows/shrinks
- Readjust every 10-20% movement
- Example: $2,000 → $2,200 (10% up) → increase stakes

**Important:** Only bet at comfortable level. If uncomfortable, scale down.

### Rule 6: Stick To It!
- Patience and discipline
- Small bank → significant amount faster than you think
- Let time, volume, and profit edge do the work
- **Impatience = guaranteed punishment**

---

## The DO's and DON'Ts

### DO:
- Establish a betting bank
- Have realistic expectations
- Use proportional staking
- Move with your bank
- Stick to your plan

### DON'T:
- Bet too much relative to bank
- Chase losses
- Change strategy during losing runs
- Get impatient
- Ignore staking rules

# PART 2: BETTING WITH RATINGS

## Creating Your Own Prices

**Step 1:** Rank horses by expected performance
**Step 2:** Determine lengths from top selection
**Step 3:** Apply score based on lengths

**Score Table (2 points per length):**

| Lengths Behind | Score |
|---------------|-------|
| 0.0 (top rated) | 1.00 |
| 0.5 | 0.85 |
| 1.0 | 0.70 |
| 1.5 | 0.60 |
| 2.0 | 0.50 |
| 2.5 | 0.43 |
| 3.0 | 0.37 |
| 4.0 | 0.27 |
| 5.0 | 0.20 |
| 6.0 | 0.15 |

**Step 4:** Calculate percentage
- Score / Total × 100 = winning chance

**Step 5:** Convert to price
- 100 / percentage = assessed price

**Example:**
- Horse A: Score 1.00, Total 3.17
- 1.00 / 3.17 = 0.31545 × 100 = 31.55%
- 100 / 31.55 = **$3.17 assessed price**

---

## The Danger of Overconfidence

**Warning:** Just because you price a horse at $3.00 doesn't make it correct.

**Key Insight:** "Race assessment is decision making in the face of incomplete information and general uncertainty."

---

## Context Tags for Prices

When you price a horse, add context:

**SHORT:** "I couldn't price the horse any shorter than I have it now"
- Positive assumptions made
- Market needs to be much shorter to consider lay

**LONG:** "I couldn't price the horse any longer than I have it now"
- Conservative assumptions
- Good betting opportunity if market is longer

**FLAT:** "Minimal uncertainty, middle of the road view"
- Balanced assessment
- Small edge may exist

**QUERY:** "High degree of guesswork built into the price"
- Uncertainty very high
- Market is better guide
- Don't bet based on your price

---

## Making Betting Decisions

**DO NOT:**
- Back horses simply because they're technical overlays
- Take $3.00 about a horse marked $2.70 if there's uncertainty
- Lay horses at $2.50 marked $3.00 without understanding assumptions

**DO:**
- Use prices as a tool
- Consider price + assumptions + market price
- Make decisions that feel right and make sense
- Feel comfortable regardless of result

**Goal:** "Use your prices to make betting decisions that make sense and feel right to you."

# PART 3: PLACE BETTING - THE SLOW BLEED

## The Reality

**Key Insight:** "If you can't make money from win betting, then you will lose more money by backing horses to place."

**Why:**
- Place betting value is significantly less than win betting
- Much more difficult to profit
- Bookmakers offer 21-22% of win odds as place price
- "Slow bleed" - more collects but inferior dividends

---

## Win vs Place Strike Rates

**Historical Data:**

| True Win Price | Actual Win % | Actual Place % | True Place Price |
|----------------|--------------|----------------|------------------|
| $2.00 | 50.0% | 81.0% | $1.23 |
| $3.00 | 33.3% | 68.0% | $1.47 |
| $4.00 | 25.0% | 59.0% | $1.69 |
| $5.00 | 20.0% | 52.0% | $1.92 |
| $7.50 | 13.3% | 41.0% | $2.44 |
| $10.00 | 10.0% | 34.0% | $2.94 |
| $15.00 | 6.7% | 26.0% | $3.85 |

---

## The Value Problem

**Example:**
- Horse at $6.00 in market
- You assess as $5.00 chance (20% win edge)
- True place price: $1.92
- Market place price: ~$2.05 (21% of 5-1 odds)
- Place edge: +6.7% (vs +20% win edge)

**Result:** You sacrifice massive value for more frequent collects.

---

## When Place Betting Works

**Exceptions to the Rule:**

1. **Dominant favourite drawn awkwardly**
   - Lower than average place ratio
   - If finds good position → in the finish
   - If gets caught wide → miss place entirely
   - Increases place appeal of other horses

2. **Consistent but less talented horse**
   - Greater than average place ratio
   - Likely one of the talented horses will win
   - But some will run below form
   - Consistent horse likely to run its race

3. **Big market mistake on longer priced horse**
   - Very confident in big edge
   - Place bet to maximise chance of collecting
   - Still make big profit with higher strike rate

---

## The Final Word on Place Betting

**"The slow bleed"** - at first doesn't seem like a big deal, but damage accumulates.

**If you want more regular collects:** Focus on shorter end of market in win betting.

**Competitive nature of win markets** makes it far easier to find value.

# PART 4: MISLEADING STATISTICS

## The Form Guide Trap

**Common beliefs that limit profits:**
- Horses not first up
- Good last start run
- Good barrier draw
- High win strike rate
- Proven at distance
- Proven at track
- Proven in track condition

**Reality:** "The tendency to make judgements by simply referring to numbers in the form guide can be very misleading and will greatly limit your potential!"

---

## Previous Wins at Distance

**Data (Metropolitan races, horses up to $10 SP):**

| Distance Form | Strike Rate | POT% |
|---------------|-------------|------|
| Never won at distance | 17.5% | -6.1% |
| Has won at distance | 17.5% | -7.2% |

**Key Finding:** Strike rate is IDENTICAL, but horses that haven't won at distance provide BETTER returns.

**Best group:** 3+ starts at distance for zero wins → **-3.3% POT**

**Worst group:** 3+ starts at distance for 3+ wins → **-11.6% POT**

**Insight:** Market overvalues obvious form. Embrace uncertainty.

---

## Previous Wins at Track

**Key Finding:** Very little difference between categories.

**Best group:** 2 starts at track for zero wins → **-0.3% POT**

**Insight:** Genuine track specialists are rare. Don't let raw statistics influence betting.

**Exception:** Consistent pattern of higher ratings at a track (even if not winning).

---

## Win Strike Rate

**The Don Scott Theory:** "The best horses to back are horses whose wins are frequent and predictable."

**Reality:** Research shows bias toward high win strike rates LIMITS profit potential.

**Data (Non-maiden metropolitan races, 10+ starts):**

| Win Strike Rate | SR% | POT% |
|-----------------|-----|------|
| 0% | 17.3% | **+48.8%** |
| 1% to 10% | 15.3% | **-2.5%** |
| 10.1% to 15% | 15.0% | **-3.1%** |
| 15.1% to 20% | 15.4% | -7.0% |
| 20.1% to 30% | 16.4% | -7.6% |
| > 30% | 19.3% | -7.5% |

**Key Finding:** Best betting returns come from horses with LOWER strike rates.

**Insight:** Market perceives low strike rate as negative → offers better price. Reality: barely a negative at all.

---

## First Up Record

**Most misleading statistic in form guide.**

**Data (First up horses up to $10 SP):**

| First Up Form | SR% | POT% |
|---------------|-----|------|
| Never won first up | 17.5% | **+3.6%** |
| Has won first up | 17.5% | **-7.6%** |

**Key Finding:** Identical strike rates, but horses that have NEVER won first up provide MUCH better returns.

**Best group:** 3+ first up runs for zero wins → **+13.8% POT**

**Worst group:** 3+ first up runs for 3+ wins → **-13.5% POT**

**Insight:** Market overvalues previous first up wins. Problem: statistics ignore class of race.

**Message:** Don't get carried away with first up statistics. Look beyond to class and quality of previous runs.

---

## Barrier Draws

**Universal belief:** Inside draw = advantage, Outside draw = disadvantage

**Reality:** Incredibly limiting belief.

**Data (Metropolitan races):**

**Races up to 1200m:**
| Barrier | SR% | POT% |
|---------|-----|------|
| 1 to 3 | 18.8% | -6.2% |
| 10 or wider | 16.0% | **-2.9%** |

**Races 1400m to 1600m:**
| Barrier | SR% | POT% |
|---------|-----|------|
| 1 to 3 | 18.1% | -4.6% |
| 10 or wider | 16.2% | **-0.4%** |

**Races 2000m+:**
| Barrier | SR% | POT% |
|---------|-----|------|
| 1 to 3 | 18.8% | -6.3% |
| 10 or wider | 14.6% | **-12.8%** |

**Key Finding:** In sprint and mile races, wide barriers provide MUCH better returns.

**Exception:** Staying races (2000m+) - wide draws return worse.

**Insight:** Barrier influence is track/distance specific. Consider racing style and speed map, not just barrier number.

**Message:** Don't be biased against wide barriers (except in staying races).

## Scalping Strategy

**Definition:** Small, consistent profits from tight spreads (1-2 ticks)

**Key Principles:**
1. **Direction matters** - follow price movement
2. **Tight spreads** - enter/exit within 1-2 ticks
3. **Quick decisions** - seconds matter
4. **High frequency** - many small trades
5. **Low risk** - always hedge

**Steam Moves:**
- Detect 5%+ price drops in 5-10 minutes
- Back at higher price
- Lay at lower price
- Exit before race starts

**AU Market Behavior:**
- Lower liquidity = slower matching
- Tighter spreads = smaller profits
- Automation essential
- Patience required

---

## Risk Management

**Golden Rules:**

1. **Never enter** if < 2 mins to race start
2. **Always exit** by 2 mins before race
3. **Exit immediately** if market goes in-play
4. **Stop trading** at end of active hours
5. **Never hold positions** into race start

**Position Sizing:**
- 1-2% of bank per trade
- Scale based on confidence
- Reduce during losing runs

**Stop Loss:**
- Exit at 2-3 ticks loss
- Don't hope for recovery
- Preserve capital
