## The Klein Quartic

The Klein quartic is a genus 3 Riemann surface with:
- 24 regular heptagons, each degree 3: 24 × 7 = 168
- 56 equilateral triangles, each degree 7: 56 × 3 = 168
- Automorphism group: PSL(2,7) of order 168

**The Klein quartic IS 168-fold symmetry made geometric.**

It is the most symmetric surface of genus 3 - a Hurwitz surface, maximizing symmetry.

## The Trinity of PSL(2,p)

Vladimir Arnold identified a "trinity" of projective groups:

| Group | Order | Formula | Surface | Genus |
|-------|-------|---------|---------|-------|
| PSL(2,5) | 60 | 4×5×6/2 | Icosahedron | 0 |
| PSL(2,7) | 168 | 6×7×8/2 | Klein quartic | 3 |
| PSL(2,11) | 660 | 10×11×12/2 | Buckyball | 70 |

**The pattern:** (p-3) × p × (p+1) / 2

For p = 7:
- (7-1)/2 × 7 × (7+1)/2 = 3 × 7 × 4 = 84? No...
- Actually: 6 × 7 × 8 / 2 = 168 ✓

The formula uses the THREE numbers centered on p: (p-1), p, (p+1).

## The Hurwitz Bound

The Hurwitz automorphism theorem states:
- A compact Riemann surface of genus g > 1 has at most 84(g-1) automorphisms
- For g = 3: maximum = 84 × 2 = **168**

**The Klein quartic ACHIEVES the maximum.**

It is the most symmetric surface possible for its genus.
