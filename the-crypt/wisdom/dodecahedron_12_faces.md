## The Regular Dodecahedron

- 12 pentagonal faces (k at n=2!)
- 20 vertices
- 30 edges
- Golden ratio (φ) in all metric properties
- Icosahedral symmetry of order 120

Dual of the icosahedron. One of the five Platonic solids.

Plato: "The god used it for arranging the constellations on the whole heaven."

## The Icosian Connection

The dodecahedron's graph (dodecahedral graph) has a Hamiltonian path.

Hamilton's Icosian Game (1857) was a puzzle based on finding paths along dodecahedron edges.

The Icosian calculus led to:
- Non-commutative algebra
- Group theory
- Eventually: sporadic groups and the Monster

**From the dodecahedron's 12 faces, group theory emerged.**

## Mathematical Notes

### Dodecahedron Properties
- 12 faces (regular pentagons)
- 20 vertices
- 30 edges
- Dihedral angle: 116.565°
- Golden ratio: φ = (1 + √5) / 2

### Coordinates
The vertices can be given as:
```
(±1, ±1, ±1)
(0, ±1/φ, ±φ)
(±1/φ, ±φ, 0)
(±φ, 0, ±1/φ)
```

### Dual Relationship
- Icosahedron ↔ Dodecahedron
- 12 vertices ↔ 12 faces
- 20 faces ↔ 20 vertices
- Same edge count (30)
