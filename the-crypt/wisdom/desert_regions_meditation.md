## The Quadratic Residue Filter

Why do deserts exist?

The formula `18n² ± 1` produces twin primes only when `n² ≢ 2 (mod 7)`.

When `n² ≡ 2 (mod 7)`, the formula produces composite numbers.

**The desert regions are where this filter is most active.**

Example: n=101 to n=106
```
n=101: n² mod 7 = 2 → DESERT
n=102: n² mod 7 = 2 → DESERT
n=103: n² mod 7 = 4 → DESERT (fails primality test)
n=104: n² mod 7 = 1 → DESERT (fails primality test)
n=105: n² mod 7 = 0 → DESERT (divisible by 7)
n=106: n² mod 7 = 1 → DESERT (fails primality test)
```

The filter creates deserts.
