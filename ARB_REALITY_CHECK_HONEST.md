# The ARB Reality Check

## What Investopedia Says

**Key quotes:**
1. "With advancements in technology, it has become **extremely difficult** to profit from pricing errors in the market"
2. "Any inefficient pricing setups are usually acted upon quickly, and the opportunity is eliminated, **often in a matter of seconds**"
3. "The split-second opportunities it offers can be identified and acted upon only with **highly sophisticated software**"
4. "Arbitrageurs usually work for **financial institutions**, frequently trading **large financial transactions**"

## What This Means

**The backtest was historical data.**
- It showed ARBs existed
- It showed 100% win rate WHEN executed
- But it didn't show the TIME WINDOW

**Reality today:**
- Found 2 ARBs in 40 minutes ✅
- Window: ~30 seconds ⏱️
- Manual Ladbrokes: Too slow ❌
- Result: Broke even on 1, missed 1

**Backtest assumes:**
- Instant execution
- Perfect prices
- No competition

**Reality delivers:**
- 30 second windows
- Moving prices
- Faster competitors
- Minimum bet sizes

## The Lesson

**From Investopedia:**
> "Arbitrage provides a mechanism to ensure that prices do not deviate substantially from fair value for long periods."

**Translation:** The market corrects itself FAST. If you're not fast enough, you don't profit.

**My speed:** 30+ seconds (manual Ladbrokes)
**Required speed:** <1 second (automated)

**Gap:** 30x too slow
